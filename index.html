Utility simulation

Choose your energy source to always fulfill demand

In the first simulation, you are taking the role of a utility planner. You choose how much of each source of electric power to build. The graph shows a week of demand. Did your choice of power plants always fulfill demand? See if you can find a low-cost, low-carbon allocation that keeps the lights on.

Policy Simulation

Choose your policies to reduce cost and CO2

Now take the role of a policymaker. You can change policies that affect utility planning. You can also change the cost assumptions for future energy sources. On this page, the utility planning is automatic: the utility planner will always keep the lights on at the least cost. See how your policies and cost assumptions affect CO2 and costs to consumers.
